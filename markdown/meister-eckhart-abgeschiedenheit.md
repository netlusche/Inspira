# Sermons
## Meister Eckhart

---

### The Nearness of the Kingdom

God is nearer to me than I am to myself; my existence depends on the nearness and presence of God. He is also near things of wood and stone, but they know it not. If a piece of wood became as aware of the nearness of God as an archangel is, the piece of wood would be as happy as an archangel.

His happiness does not arise from this that God is near him — but from this, that he knows the nearness of God, and loves Him.

God is equally near in all creatures. He knows God rightly who recognizes Him alike in all things.

---

### On True Hearing

Three things hinder us from hearing the everlasting Word. The first is fleshliness, the second is distraction, the third is the illusion of time. If a man could get free of these, he would dwell in eternity, and in the spirit, and in the desert, and there would hear the everlasting Word.

The eye with which I see God is the same with which God sees me. My eye and God's eye is one eye, and one sight, and one knowledge, and one love.

---

### On Freedom and Love

God is always ready, but we are very unready. God is near us, but we are far from Him. God is within, and we are without. God is friendly; we are estranged.

To serve God with fear is good; to serve Him out of love is better; but to fear and love Him together is best of all. To have a restful or peaceful life in God is good; to bear a life of pain in patience is better; but to have peace in the midst of pain is the best of all.

---

### On the Soul's Journey

If the soul is to know God, it must know Him outside time and place, since God is neither in this or that, but One and above them.

When it has lost itself and everything in God, it finds itself again in God when it attains to the knowledge of Him, and it finds also everything which it had abandoned complete in God.

The aim of man is beyond the temporal — in the serene region of the everlasting Present.
